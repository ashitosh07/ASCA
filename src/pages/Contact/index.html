Advance Tax

Advance Tax is a system where taxes are paid in the same year income is received, embodying the 'pay-as-you-earn' principle. It is mandated by the Income Tax Department to ensure a smooth flow of tax payments from taxpayers, who are required to make these payments quarterly. This prepayment of tax is known as Advance Tax.

Understanding Advance Tax

1.1 Definition of Advance Tax

Advance tax is a mechanism designed to facilitate a consistent inflow of tax revenue into the government's treasury without waiting for the tax liability to be finalized at the end of the fiscal year. Under this system, taxpayers must estimate their current income and pay taxes in instalments throughout the fiscal year if their estimated tax exceeds a certain threshold. This approach allows for the payment of taxes concurrently with income generation, hence the alternate name 'Pay as You Earn Scheme'.

1.2 Eligibility for Paying Advance Tax

All taxpayers whose estimated tax liability for the financial year exceeds ₹10,000 must pay advance tax, regardless of their residential status.

1.2-1 Exemption Criteria

Individual residents who are 60 years or older and do not earn income from business or professional endeavours are exempt from paying advance tax.

1.3 Calculating Advance Tax

Advance tax applies to 'current income' that will be taxed in the following assessment year. All incomes are subject to advance tax, even if subject to tax deduction at source or special tax rates. To compute advance tax, estimate the total income for the fiscal year and calculate the tax liability accordingly, applying the rates specified for the relevant assessment year.

1.4 Payment Schedule for Advance Tax

Advance tax is paid in four instalments within specified dates throughout the fiscal year. Specific provisions are made for taxpayers under the presumptive tax scheme, allowing them to pay their entire advance tax liability in one instalment by March 15.

1.5 Adjustments in Advance Tax Payments

Taxpayers can adjust their advance tax payments in subsequent instalments if they have paid too much or too little in earlier payments.

1.6 Payment Methods for Advance Tax

Advance tax can be paid electronically through Internet banking or directly at authorized banks. Certain rules apply for electronic payments and payments through other people's accounts, with clear guidelines on the use of PAN for transactions.

1.7 Non-compliance Penalties

Failure to pay advance tax or to adhere to the payment schedule results in interest penalties under Sections 234B and 234C.

Advance Tax Notices from the Assessing Officer

2.1 Issuance of Advance Tax Notice

The Assessing Officer (AO) may issue a notice for advance tax payment based on previous assessments or if deemed necessary, specifying the amount and installment schedule.

2.1-1 Involvement of Agents for Non-residents

Agents of non-residents may be required to pay advance tax if designated by the AO after a hearing process.

2.2 Deadline for Advance Tax Notice

Notices for advance tax payment can be issued anytime within the financial year but no later than the last day of February.

2.3 Determining Advance Tax by the AO

The AO calculates the advance tax based on the higher income between the latest assessed year and any disclosed subsequent income, including adjustments for tax deducted or collected at source.

2.4 Responding to Advance Tax Demand

Upon receiving a demand notice, taxpayers can adjust their payment based on their current income estimation and are not required to submit detailed calculations to the AO.

2.5 Consequences of Non-payment

Non-compliance with advance tax demand notices deems the taxpayer as defaulting, incurring penalties.

2.6 Credit for Advance Tax Paid

Payments made towards advance tax are credited against the taxpayer's current income liability during regular assessment.
